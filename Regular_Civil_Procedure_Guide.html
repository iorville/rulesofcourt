PART I: JURISDICTION OF COURTS

A. Republic Act No. 11576 (2021) - Key Changes

MAJOR CHANGE: RA 11576 (effective October 7, 2022) UNIFIED jurisdictional amounts nationwide. There is NO longer any distinction between Metro Manila and outside Metro Manila courts.

Effectivity: October 7, 2022 (15 days after complete publication)

Key Provisions of RA 11576:

  1. Uniform Jurisdiction - Eliminated Metro Manila vs. Outside Metro Manila distinction
  2. Increased Thresholds - Raised jurisdictional amounts for first-level courts
  3. Automatic Adjustment - Jurisdictional amounts adjust every 5 years based on inflation
  4. Delegated Small Claims - OCA may adjust small claims threshold (currently ₱1,000,000)

B. First-Level Courts (MTC/MTCC/MeTC/MCTC)

Types of First-Level Courts:

Court Location
MeTC - Metropolitan Trial Court Metro Manila
MTCC - Municipal Trial Court in Cities Cities outside Metro Manila
MTC - Municipal Trial Court Municipalities
MCTC - Municipal Circuit Trial Court Circuit of municipalities

CIVIL Jurisdiction (RA 11576):

Type of Case Jurisdictional Amount Legal Basis
Personal Actions (money claims, damages) ≤ ₱2,000,000 RA 11576, Sec. 1(1)
Real Actions (recovery of property) Assessed Value: ≤ ₱400,000 RA 11576, Sec. 1(3)
Admiralty/Maritime (no vessel) ≤ ₱2,000,000 RA 11576, Sec. 1(4)
Probate (gross estate value) ≤ ₱2,000,000 RA 11576, Sec. 1(5)

EXCLUSIVE Original Jurisdiction (Regardless of Amount):

Case Type Notes
Forcible Entry Filed within 1 year from dispossession
Unlawful Detainer Filed within 1 year from last demand
Small Claims ≤ ₱1,000,000 (A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC)
Summary Procedure Cases As covered by A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC
Barangay Conciliation Matters LGC Sec. 399-422
Memory Note (Criminal - Reference Only): First-level courts also have jurisdiction over offenses punishable by imprisonment ≤ 6 years (regardless of fine), and all offenses involving damage to property through criminal negligence. This guide focuses on civil procedure.

Appellate Jurisdiction:

  • Petition for Review under Rule 42 from MTC decisions (questions of law only)

Designated Special RTC Courts:

Designation Cases Covered

Intellectual Property Courts Patent, trademark, copyright infringement
Environmental Courts Environmental protection, Writ of Kalikasan
Family Courts Family law matters (RA 8369)
Memory Note (Criminal - Reference Only): RTC has exclusive jurisdiction over offenses punishable by imprisonment > 6 years. Special Drug Courts handle RA 9165 cases. Cybercrime Courts handle RA 10175 cases.

D. Court of Appeals

Original Jurisdiction:

  • Certiorari, Prohibition, Mandamus against RTC
  • Habeas Corpus, Habeas Data, Writ of Amparo
  • Quo Warranto (in aid of appellate jurisdiction)
  • Annulment of RTC judgments

Appellate Jurisdiction:

Petition for Review RTC (appellate jurisdiction) 15 days Rule 42
Petition for Review Quasi-judicial agencies (NLRC, DARAB, etc.) 15 days Rule 43

E. Supreme Court

Original Jurisdiction:

  • Certiorari, Prohibition, Mandamus against CA, COMELEC, COA, Sandiganbayan
  • Disciplinary cases against judges and lawyers
  • Cases affecting ambassadors, public ministers, consuls

Appellate Jurisdiction:

Mode Cases Period Rule
Petition for Review on Certiorari CA decisions (questions of law) 15 days Rule 45
Appeal by Certiorari CTA en banc decisions 15 days Rule 45
Petition for Review Sandiganbayan civil cases 15 days Rule 45

  • Findings of fact of CA are generally FINAL
  • Discretionary review (may be denied without explanation)
  • Must show special and important reasons for acceptance

PART II: VENUE

A. General Rules on Venue (Rule 4)

Type of Action Venue Rule
Real Actions
(affecting title/possession of real property)		 Where the property or any portion thereof is situated Rule 4, Sec. 1
Personal Actions
(all others)		 Residence of plaintiff OR defendant, at plaintiff's election Rule 4, Sec. 2
Non-Resident Defendant Where plaintiff resides OR where defendant may be found Rule 4, Sec. 2

B. Venue Stipulations

2019 Amendment (Rule 4, Sec. 4):
Written stipulation on venue is RESTRICTIVE only when it EXPRESSLY states that the agreed venue is the "EXCLUSIVE" or "ONLY" venue. Otherwise, it is merely additional.

C. Venue vs. Jurisdiction

Aspect Venue Jurisdiction
Nature Procedural Substantive
Waivable? YES (if not objected timely) NO (cannot be conferred by agreement)
When to object In answer (affirmative defense) or motion to dismiss Any stage, even on appeal
Stipulation May be agreed upon Cannot be stipulated

PART III: PARTIES TO CIVIL ACTIONS

A. Real Party in Interest (Rule 3, Sec. 2)

One who stands to be benefited or injured by the judgment, or entitled to the avails of the suit.

B. Capacity to Sue and Be Sued

Entity Capacity Representative
Natural persons Full legal capacity None needed
Minors/Incompetents Through guardian or guardian ad litem Rule 3, Sec. 5
Juridical persons (corporations) Through authorized officer Board resolution required
Unincorporated associations Through authorized representative Must show authority
Estate of deceased Through executor/administrator Rule 3, Sec. 16

C. Indispensable and Necessary Parties

Type Definition Effect of Non-Joinder
Indispensable Without whom no final determination can be had Case must be DISMISSED
Necessary Ought to be joined for complete relief but not indispensable Case proceeds; party may be added

D. Class Suit (Rule 3, Sec. 12)

Requirements:

  1. Subject matter of controversy is of common or general interest to many persons
  2. Parties are so numerous that joinder is impracticable
  3. Parties bringing suit are sufficiently numerous and representative
  4. Representatives fairly and adequately protect interests of all

PART IV: PLEADINGS

A. Kinds of Pleadings (Rule 6)

Pleading Purpose Period
Complaint Initiates action; states cause of action Within prescriptive period
Answer Responds to complaint; raises defenses 30 days from service
Counterclaim Defendant's claim against plaintiff With the Answer
Cross-claim Claim against co-party With the Answer
Reply Responds to new matters in answer 15 days from service
Third-Party Complaint Defendant's claim against non-party With leave of court

A. Complaint (Rule 6, Sec. 3; Rule 8)

Required Contents:

  1. Caption - Name of court, title of action, docket number
  2. Body
    • Names and addresses of parties
    • Material facts constituting cause of action (ultimate facts)
    • Specific relief sought
  3. Signature and Address of counsel or party
  4. Verification (if required)
  5. Certification against Forum Shopping

2019 Amendment - Certification Against Forum Shopping (Rule 7, Sec. 5):

Who Signs:
  • Plaintiff or principal party
  • If corporation: specifically authorized officer/agent (attach board resolution)
  • Counsel CANNOT sign (except with SPA specifically authorizing)
Effect of Non-Compliance: Failure is NOT curable by amendment; cause for DISMISSAL

Annexes to Complaint:

  • Actionable documents (contracts, deeds, etc.)
  • Barangay certification (if applicable)
  • Tax declaration and tax receipts (real actions)
  • Secretary's certificate/Board resolution (corporations)
  • Special Power of Attorney (if representative files)

B. Answer (Rule 6, Sec. 4; Rule 11)

2019 AMENDMENT - PERIOD TO FILE ANSWER:
  • Regular procedure: 30 CALENDAR DAYS from service of summons
  • Summary procedure: 10 days
  • Small claims: 10 days (Response)
  • Defendant outside Philippines: 60 days minimum

Contents of Answer:

  1. Specific Denials - Must specify which allegations are denied and state basis
    • Specific denial
    • Denial of knowledge/information sufficient to form belief
    • Denial by disavowal (documents)
  2. Affirmative Defenses (Rule 8, Sec. 5)
  3. Counterclaim (if any)
  4. Cross-claim (if any)

Affirmative Defenses (2019 Amendments):

Defense Effect if Proven
Lack of jurisdiction over subject matter Dismissal
Lack of jurisdiction over person Dismissal (waivable)
Improper venue Dismissal (waivable)
Plaintiff lacks legal capacity Dismissal
Litis pendentia Dismissal
Res judicata Dismissal
Prescription Dismissal
Failure to comply with barangay conciliation Dismissal without prejudice
Failure to state cause of action Dismissal
2019 AMENDMENT - NO MORE MOTION TO DISMISS (Generally):
Rule 16 was substantially amended. Grounds for dismissal are now raised as AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES in the Answer, except:
  • Lack of jurisdiction over subject matter
  • Litis pendentia
  • Res judicata
  • Prescription
These four may still be subject of a motion to dismiss filed within 30 days from service of summons.

Effect of Failure to File Answer:

Defendant may be declared in DEFAULT upon motion (Rule 9, Sec. 3)

C. Counterclaims and Cross-Claims (Rule 6, Secs. 7-8)

Type Definition Requirements
Compulsory Counterclaim Arises out of same transaction/occurrence; does not require third parties; within court's jurisdiction MUST be raised or deemed waived; no filing fee
Permissive Counterclaim Does not arise from same transaction; independent claim Filing fee required; may be filed separately
Cross-Claim Claim by one party against co-party arising from same transaction Must arise from subject matter of original action

D. Reply (Rule 6, Sec. 10)

  • Period: 15 days from service of answer
  • Purpose: To deny new matters alleged in answer
  • If no reply filed: New matters deemed CONTROVERTED

E. Third-Party Complaint (Rule 6, Sec. 11)

  • Filed by defendant against person not party to action
  • For contribution, indemnity, subrogation, or other relief
  • Requires leave of court
  • Third-party defendant may file answer within 15 days

F. Amendments to Pleadings (Rule 10)

Type When Allowed Requirement
Amendment as Matter of Right Before responsive pleading is served; or within 30 days after Once, without leave
Amendment by Leave After period for amendment as of right Motion; leave of court
Amendment to Conform to Evidence When issues not in pleadings are tried with consent May amend at any time
Supplemental Pleading For facts arising after original pleading Leave of court; Rule 10, Sec. 6

PART V: SUMMONS

A. Purpose of Summons

  1. Acquire jurisdiction over the person of defendant
  2. Notify defendant of action against them
  3. Afford opportunity to be heard

B. Modes of Service (Rule 14)

Mode How Made Priority
Personal Service Handing copy to defendant in person FIRST PRIORITY
Substituted Service If personal service fails after reasonable efforts: serve on person of suitable age/discretion at residence OR competent person in charge at office SECOND (requires showing of failed personal service)
Service by Publication Publication in newspaper + copy sent to last known address For actions in rem/quasi in rem; unknown whereabouts
Extraterritorial Service Personal service abroad; publication; other means court orders For non-resident defendants (Rule 14, Sec. 17)

2019 Amendment - Service on Entities:

Entity Who to Serve
Domestic Private Corporation President, managing partner, general manager, corporate secretary, treasurer, or in-house counsel
Foreign Private Corporation Resident agent designated by law; or government official designated by law
Republic of the Philippines Solicitor General
LGU or Government Agency Executive head or other officer designated by law
IMPORTANT - Strict Compliance Required:
Service of summons must be made personally first. Substituted service is allowed ONLY upon sworn statement that personal service was attempted on at least 3 dates on 2 different occasions and failed.

PART VI: MOTIONS

A. Definition and Requirements (Rule 15)

A motion is an application for relief other than by a pleading.

Requirements for Written Motions:

  1. In writing (except those made in open court)
  2. State relief sought
  3. State grounds relied upon
  4. Accompanied by supporting affidavits and documents
  5. Notice of hearing (if required)

B. Litigious vs Non-Litigious Motions (2019 Amendments)

2019 AMENDMENT - Simplified Motion Procedure:
Motions are now classified as LITIGIOUS or NON-LITIGIOUS. This determines hearing requirements.
Litigious Motions Non-Litigious Motions
  • Motion for new trial
  • Motion for reconsideration
  • Motion for execution pending appeal
  • Motion to dismiss (when allowed)
  • Motion to quash
  • Motion for bill of particulars
  • Motion to resolve affirmative defenses
  • Motion for extension of time
  • Motion for postponement
  • Motion to admit answer
  • Motion to substitute counsel
  • Motion for inhibition
  • Motion for intervention
  • Other motions not affecting rights of adverse party
REQUIRE hearing and 3-day notice May be acted upon without hearing; 5-day notice

C. 3-Day Notice Rule (Litigious Motions)

  • Notice of hearing must be served at least 3 days before hearing
  • Must specify time and date of hearing
  • Motion without proper notice = mere scrap of paper

D. Omnibus Motion Rule (Rule 15, Sec. 8)

Rule: A motion attacking a pleading must include ALL grounds then available; grounds not raised are deemed WAIVED.

Exceptions (never waived):
  • Lack of jurisdiction over subject matter
  • Litis pendentia
  • Res judicata
  • Prescription

PART VII: DISMISSAL OF ACTIONS

A. Dismissal Upon Notice (Rule 17, Sec. 1)

  • Plaintiff may dismiss by filing notice before service of answer or motion for summary judgment
  • Effect: Without prejudice (generally)
  • If counterclaim has been filed: requires counterclaim be dismissed also OR prosecuted independently

B. Dismissal by Order of Court (Rule 17, Sec. 3)

Court may dismiss upon motion or motu proprio for:

  1. Failure to prosecute for unreasonable length of time
  2. Failure to comply with rules or court order
  3. Failure to appear on pre-trial

Effect: WITH prejudice (adjudication on merits) unless otherwise stated

C. Two-Dismissal Rule

If plaintiff previously dismissed in ANY court an action based on the SAME cause of action, a second dismissal operates as adjudication on the merits (with prejudice).

D. Effect of Dismissal on Affirmative Defenses

Ground Effect
Lack of jurisdiction Without prejudice (may refile in proper court)
Improper venue Without prejudice (may refile in proper venue)
Failure to state cause of action Without prejudice (may amend or refile)
Prescription With prejudice
Res judicata With prejudice
Litis pendentia Without prejudice (pending case determines)

PART VIII: PRE-TRIAL

A. When Conducted (Rule 18)

  • After last pleading is filed
  • Court issues pre-trial order setting date
  • Notice must be served at least 3 days before pre-trial

B. Pre-Trial Brief (Rule 18, Sec. 6)

2019 Amendment: Pre-trial brief must be filed at least 3 DAYS before pre-trial (previously 1 day).

Contents of Pre-Trial Brief:

  1. Statement of willingness to enter into amicable settlement
  2. Summary of admitted facts and proposed stipulations
  3. Issues to be tried or resolved
  4. Documents or exhibits to be presented (with copies attached)
  5. Manifestation of use of testimonies through judicial affidavits
  6. Number and names of witnesses with summary of testimony
  7. All other matters required by law

C. Matters to be Considered at Pre-Trial (Rule 18, Sec. 2)

  1. Possibility of amicable settlement or submission to ADR
  2. Simplification of issues
  3. Necessity or desirability of amendments to pleadings
  4. Possibility of obtaining stipulations of facts and documents
  5. Limitation of number of witnesses
  6. Advisability of preliminary reference to commissioner
  7. Propriety of rendering judgment on pleadings or summary judgment
  8. Such other matters as may aid in prompt disposition

D. Effect of Failure to Appear or File Pre-Trial Brief

Party Effect of Non-Appearance Effect of No Pre-Trial Brief
Plaintiff DISMISSAL with prejudice DISMISSAL with prejudice
Defendant Plaintiff may present evidence ex parte Same effect

E. Pre-Trial Order (Rule 18, Sec. 7)

Within 10 days after pre-trial, court issues Pre-Trial Order containing:

  • Actions taken at pre-trial
  • Facts admitted/stipulated
  • Issues identified
  • Documents marked
  • Names of witnesses

Effect: Pre-trial order CONTROLS the trial; issues/evidence not included are deemed waived (except with good cause shown)

F. Court-Annexed Mediation (CAM) and Judicial Dispute Resolution (JDR)

ADR Requirement: Before pre-trial, court shall refer parties to:
  1. Court-Annexed Mediation (CAM) - With accredited mediator (mandatory for most civil cases)
  2. Judicial Dispute Resolution (JDR) - If CAM fails, judge (other than trial judge) conducts JDR
Only after CAM and JDR fail will case proceed to pre-trial and trial.

PART IX: INTERVENTION

A. Requirements (Rule 19)

  1. Legal interest in the matter in litigation
  2. Success of either party will not prejudice intervenor
  3. Intervenor's interest not adequately represented
  4. Will not unduly delay or prejudice rights of original parties

B. When to Intervene

At any time BEFORE rendition of judgment by trial court.

C. Procedure

  • Motion for leave to intervene
  • Attach pleading-in-intervention (complaint or answer)
  • Court resolves motion

PART X: SUBPOENA

A. Kinds of Subpoena (Rule 21)

Type Purpose
Subpoena ad testificandum Compel attendance of witness to give testimony
Subpoena duces tecum Compel production of documents or things

B. Viatorial Right (Mileage)

Witness served with subpoena is entitled to:

  • Kilometrage - ₱1.50 per kilometer (from residence to court)
  • Attendance fee - ₱100 per day

C. Quashing Subpoena (Rule 21, Sec. 4)

Court may quash subpoena if:

  1. Unreasonable or oppressive
  2. Privileged matter
  3. Witness fees not tendered
  4. Documentary evidence not described with particularity

PART XI: MODES OF DISCOVERY

A. Overview (Rules 23-29)

Mode Description Rule
Deposition Upon Oral Examination Witness examined orally; testimony recorded Rule 23
Deposition Upon Written Interrogatories Written questions submitted; answers transcribed Rule 24
Written Interrogatories to Parties Written questions to adverse party; answered under oath Rule 25
Request for Admission Request to admit facts or genuineness of documents Rule 26
Production/Inspection of Documents Compel production of documents or things; permit entry on land Rule 27

B. Request for Admission (Rule 26)

Effect of Failure to Respond:
If party fails to respond to Request for Admission within 15 days, the matters are deemed ADMITTED.

C. Sanctions for Non-Compliance (Rule 29)

  • Order to compel
  • Contempt
  • Striking out pleadings
  • Staying proceedings until compliance
  • Dismissal or rendering judgment by default

PART XII: TRIAL

A. Judicial Affidavit Rule (A.M. No. 12-8-8-SC)

General Rule: Testimony of witnesses in civil cases shall be in the form of JUDICIAL AFFIDAVITS.

Exception: Does not apply to:
  • Small claims cases
  • Cases covered by Rules on Summary Procedure
  • Child witnesses (special rule applies)

Contents of Judicial Affidavit:

  1. Name, age, residence, occupation of witness
  2. Name and address of lawyer who conducted examination
  3. Place and date where examination took place
  4. Testimony in Q&A form
  5. Signature of witness
  6. Jurat (notarization)
  7. Attestation of lawyer

When to Submit:

Judicial affidavits and documentary evidence shall be submitted not later than 5 days before pre-trial (or as scheduled by court)

B. Order of Trial (Rule 30)

  1. Plaintiff presents evidence (direct examination through JA, cross-examination)
  2. Defendant presents evidence
  3. Third-party defendant, if any
  4. Parties may present rebuttal evidence
  5. Sur-rebuttal (if allowed)

C. Continuous Trial (A.M. No. 21-06-08-SC)

Guidelines for Continuous Trial:
  • Trial should be completed within prescribed period
  • Hearing dates must be consecutive or close together
  • Postponements are discouraged
  • Cross-examination immediately follows direct examination

D. Judgment on the Pleadings (Rule 34)

When answer fails to tender an issue (admits material allegations), court may render judgment on the pleadings upon motion.

E. Summary Judgment (Rule 35)

When there is no genuine issue as to any material fact, court may render summary judgment based on:

  • Pleadings
  • Depositions
  • Admissions
  • Affidavits
For Claimant For Defendant
No defense to all or part of claim No genuine issue as to any material fact; entitled to judgment as matter of law

PART XIII: JUDGMENT

A. Rendition of Judgment (Rule 36)

Constitutional Requirement (Art. VIII, Sec. 15):
  • Lower courts: Decide within 3 MONTHS from date of submission
  • Court of Appeals: Decide within 12 MONTHS
  • Supreme Court: Decide within 24 MONTHS

B. Contents of Judgment

  1. Statement of case
  2. Clear statement of facts (proven and admitted)
  3. Law upon which decision is based
  4. Dispositive portion (fallo) - the ruling

C. Entry of Judgment

  • After judgment becomes final and executory
  • Recorded in book of entries of judgment
  • Date of finality noted

D. Doctrine of Finality (Immutability of Judgments)

A final and executory judgment can no longer be modified, even if erroneous, except:

  1. Clerical errors
  2. Nunc pro tunc entries
  3. Void judgments
  4. Supervening events

E. Kinds of Judgments

Type Description
Judgment on the Merits After trial on facts; conclusive on issues
Default Judgment Against party who failed to answer or appear
Summary Judgment When no genuine issue of fact
Judgment on Pleadings When answer admits all material allegations
Judgment on Compromise Based on agreement of parties
Partial Judgment Decides some but not all claims/parties

PART XIV: POST-JUDGMENT REMEDIES

A. Motion for Reconsideration (MR) - Rule 37

Aspect Details
Period 15 days from notice of judgment
Grounds Errors of law or fact; newly discovered evidence
Pro Forma MR Does NOT toll period to appeal
Limit Only ONE MR allowed

B. Motion for New Trial - Rule 37

Aspect Details
Period 15 days from notice of judgment
Grounds (a) FAME - Fraud, Accident, Mistake, Excusable negligence
(b) Newly discovered evidence
Requirements for NDE (1) Discovered after trial
(2) Could not have been discovered with reasonable diligence
(3) Material - would change outcome
(4) Not cumulative or corroborative

C. Relief from Judgment (Rule 38)

Periods (BOTH must be met):
  • Within 60 days from knowledge of judgment/order
  • AND within 6 months from entry of judgment
Grounds: FAME (when there was no participation in proceedings)

D. Annulment of Judgment (Rule 47)

Filed with Court of Appeals against RTC judgments.

Ground Period
Extrinsic fraud 4 years from discovery
Lack of jurisdiction Before barred by laches or estoppel
Prerequisites:
  • Ordinary remedies (MR, appeal, relief) no longer available through no fault of petitioner
  • Cannot be used as substitute for lost appeal

PART XV: EXECUTION

A. When Execution Issues (Rule 39)

Type When Requirement
Execution as a Matter of Right After judgment becomes final and executory Motion; writ issues as matter of course
Discretionary Execution Before finality (pending appeal) Good reasons stated in special order; motion with notice and hearing

B. Execution by Motion vs. Independent Action

By Motion By Independent Action
Within 5 years from entry of judgment After 5 years but within 10 years from entry
Same case, same court Separate action to revive judgment

C. Procedure of Execution (Rule 39, Sec. 9)

For Money Judgment:

  1. Demand - Sheriff demands immediate payment
  2. Levy on Personal Property - If debtor cannot pay; garnishment of credits
  3. Levy on Real Property - If personal property insufficient
  4. Sale at Public Auction - After notice; proceeds applied to judgment

For Specific Acts:

  • Delivery of property
  • Execution of conveyances
  • Performance of specific act (court may perform or third party at cost of disobedient party)

D. Exempt Properties (Rule 39, Sec. 13)

Properties EXEMPT from Execution:
  1. Family home (subject to value limits)
  2. Necessary clothing
  3. Furniture/utensils necessary for housekeeping
  4. Provisions for individual/family (4 months)
  5. Professional libraries and equipment
  6. Tools/implements for trade (2 months income)
  7. One fishing boat and accessories
  8. Lettered gravestones
  9. Benefits from SSS, GSIS
  10. Right to receive support
  11. Money benefits under insurance

E. Third-Party Claim (Terceria)

When property levied is claimed by third person:

  1. Third party files affidavit of ownership
  2. Sheriff notifies judgment creditor
  3. Creditor must file indemnity bond to proceed
  4. Or creditor may file action for recovery

F. Redemption

Who May Redeem Period
Judgment debtor Within 1 year from registration of sale
Successor in interest Within 1 year from registration of sale
Redemptioner (creditor with lien) Within 1 year from registration OR within 60 days from last redemption

PART XVI: APPEALS

A. Appeal Routes

From To Mode Period Rule
MTC (original) RTC Notice of Appeal 15 days Rule 40
RTC (original) CA Notice of Appeal 15 days Rule 41
RTC (appellate) CA Petition for Review 15 days Rule 42
Quasi-Judicial (CTA, NLRC, DARAB, etc.) CA Petition for Review 15 days Rule 43
CA SC Petition for Review on Certiorari 15 days Rule 45

B. Notice of Appeal (Rules 40 & 41)

Requirements:
  1. Filed within 15 days from notice of judgment
  2. If MR filed: 15 days from notice of denial
  3. Payment of docket and other lawful fees
  4. Filed with court that rendered judgment
Perfection: Upon filing of notice + payment of fees (timely)

C. Record on Appeal (Rule 41, Sec. 2)

Required only in:

  • Special proceedings
  • Multiple appeals (partial judgment)
  • Other cases as required by law

Period: 30 days from notice of judgment (may be extended)

D. Petition for Review (Rule 42)

From RTC (in its appellate jurisdiction) to CA

Contents:

  1. Full names of parties
  2. Material dates (judgment, MR, denial)
  3. Statement of matters involved
  4. Questions raised
  5. Reasons for review
  6. Verification and certification against forum shopping

E. Petition for Review on Certiorari (Rule 45)

From CA to Supreme Court

STRICT Requirements:
  • Only QUESTIONS OF LAW
  • 15 days from notice (extendible for 30 days for compelling reasons)
  • Discretionary - SC may refuse without explanation
  • Must show CA committed reversible error

F. Questions of Law vs. Fact

Question of Law Question of Fact
What is the applicable law? What really happened?
How should the law be interpreted? Is the evidence credible?
Did the court correctly apply the law? Are the findings supported by evidence?
Reviewable on Rule 45 Generally NOT reviewable on Rule 45

G. Effect of Appeal

  • Perfected appeal stays execution (unless execution pending appeal)
  • Trial court loses jurisdiction over case
  • Appellate court acquires jurisdiction

H. Dismissal of Appeal

Grounds:

  1. Failure to file on time
  2. Failure to pay docket fees
  3. Failure to comply with requirements (brief, etc.)
  4. No reversible error apparent
  5. Abandonment

A. Overview (Rules 62-71)

Declaratory Relief 63 Determine rights under deed, will, contract, statute before breach
Review of Judgments of COMELEC and COA 64 Certiorari to SC
Certiorari 65 Correct grave abuse of discretion amounting to lack of jurisdiction
Prohibition 65 Prevent tribunal from acting without/excess jurisdiction
Mandamus 65 Compel performance of ministerial duty
Quo Warranto 66 Challenge right to hold public office or franchise
Expropriation 67 Government taking of private property for public use
Foreclosure of Mortgage 68 Enforce security over property
Partition 69 Divide property among co-owners
Forcible Entry & Unlawful Detainer 70 Recover possession of property
Contempt 71 Punish disobedience to court

B. Certiorari, Prohibition, and Mandamus (Rule 65)

CRITICAL PERIOD: 60 DAYS from notice of judgment, order, or resolution

This period is STRICT and NON-EXTENDIBLE (Laguna Metts case doctrine)
Remedy Grounds Relief
Certiorari Grave abuse of discretion amounting to lack/excess of jurisdiction Annul or modify proceedings
Prohibition Tribunal acting without/excess jurisdiction Prevent tribunal from proceeding
Mandamus Tribunal unlawfully neglects ministerial duty; no other adequate remedy Compel performance

Where to File:

  • Against MTC → RTC
  • Against RTC → CA
  • Against CA, COMELEC, COA → SC
  • Against quasi-judicial bodies → CA (generally)

Requisites for Certiorari:

  1. Tribunal exercised judicial or quasi-judicial function
  2. Acted without or in excess of jurisdiction, or with grave abuse of discretion
  3. No appeal or other plain, speedy, and adequate remedy
  4. Filed within 60 days

C. Forcible Entry and Unlawful Detainer (Rule 70)

Aspect Forcible Entry Unlawful Detainer
How possession was acquired By force, intimidation, strategy, threat, stealth (FISTS) Lawfully at first (by contract, tolerance)
Demand required? NO YES
Period to file Within 1 year from dispossession/discovery Within 1 year from last demand to vacate
Court with jurisdiction First-level courts (EXCLUSIVE regardless of value)
Procedure Summary Procedure (Rule 70 + A.M. 08-8-7-SC)

Immediate Execution (Rule 70, Sec. 19)

To Stay Execution During Appeal:
  1. Perfect appeal
  2. File supersedeas bond (reasonable rentals + damages)
  3. Deposit periodically rentals/reasonable compensation during appeal
Failure to comply = immediate execution

D. Expropriation (Rule 67)

Procedure:

  1. Government files complaint for expropriation
  2. Deposit required (Rule 67, Sec. 2): BIR zonal value OR if none, assessed value
  3. Upon deposit, government may take possession
  4. Order of expropriation (determination of public use/necessity)
  5. Appointment of commissioners to determine just compensation
  6. Court determines just compensation based on commissioners' report
  7. Payment of just compensation

E. Partition (Rule 69)

When co-owners cannot agree on division:

  1. Complaint for partition
  2. Court orders partition if proper
  3. If divisible: physical division among co-owners
  4. If indivisible: sale at public auction, proceeds divided

PART XVIII: PROVISIONAL REMEDIES

A. Overview (Rules 57-61)

Remedy Rule Purpose
Preliminary Attachment 57 Secure satisfaction of judgment by attaching debtor's property
Preliminary Injunction 58 Preserve status quo; prevent irreparable injury
Receivership 59 Preserve property during litigation
Replevin 60 Recover specific personal property
Support Pendente Lite 61 Secure support during litigation

B. Preliminary Attachment (Rule 57)

Grounds:

  1. Defendant about to depart Philippines with intent to defraud
  2. Defendant about to remove/dispose property with intent to defraud
  3. Defendant guilty of fraud in contracting debt
  4. Defendant has removed/disposed property with intent to defraud
  5. Defendant is a non-resident or foreign corporation
  6. Action for money/damages arising from delict or quasi-delict

Requirements:

  1. Verified application
  2. Affidavit showing grounds
  3. Bond in amount fixed by court

C. Preliminary Injunction (Rule 58)

Types:

Type Duration Requirements
Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) Effective for 20 days (72 hours if ex parte) Great and irreparable injury; matter of extreme urgency
Preliminary Injunction Until further orders of court Notice and hearing; bond required
Permanent Injunction Perpetual (after trial) Final adjudication of rights

Requisites:

  1. Clear and unmistakable right to be protected
  2. Material and substantial invasion of such right
  3. Urgent need to prevent irreparable injury
  4. No adequate remedy at law

D. Replevin (Rule 60)

Recovery of personal property wrongfully detained.

Procedure:

  1. Verified complaint
  2. Affidavit showing: (a) plaintiff is owner or entitled to possession; (b) property is wrongfully detained; (c) not taken for tax assessment; (d) actual value of property
  3. Bond = double the value of property
  4. Sheriff takes property
  5. Defendant may file counter-bond to retain possession

PART XXI: CHECKLISTS

A. Complaint Filing Checklist

Before Filing - Verify:

  • ☐ Proper court (jurisdiction - subject matter and amount)
  • ☐ Proper venue (real action: property location; personal: residence)
  • ☐ Prescription period not expired
  • ☐ Barangay conciliation completed (if required)
  • ☐ Real party in interest / Capacity to sue
  • ☐ No litis pendentia or res judicata

Complaint Contents:

  • ☐ Caption (court, title, docket number space)
  • ☐ Names and addresses of all parties
  • ☐ Statement of material/ultimate facts
  • ☐ Cause of action clearly stated
  • ☐ Specific relief sought
  • ☐ Prayer
  • ☐ Signature of counsel/party
  • ☐ MCLE compliance number (if counsel)

Annexes:

  • ☐ Verification (sworn statement of truth)
  • ☐ Certification Against Forum Shopping (by plaintiff, NOT counsel)
  • ☐ Actionable documents (contracts, deeds)
  • ☐ Barangay Certificate to File Action (if applicable)
  • ☐ Tax declaration/receipts (real actions)
  • ☐ Board resolution/Secretary's certificate (corporations)
  • ☐ Special Power of Attorney (if representative)

Filing:

  • ☐ Original + required copies
  • ☐ Docket fees paid
  • ☐ Filing fees for claims computed correctly
  • ☐ Receive stamped copy with docket number

B. Answer Preparation Checklist

Before Drafting - Note:

  • ☐ Date summons was received/served
  • ☐ Deadline: 30 days from service (regular); 10 days (summary)
  • ☐ Review complaint thoroughly
  • ☐ Identify all allegations requiring response

Answer Contents:

  • ☐ Caption (matching complaint)
  • ☐ Specific denials (paragraph by paragraph)
    • ☐ Specify which allegations are denied
    • ☐ State basis for denial
    • ☐ Denial for lack of knowledge (where applicable)
  • ☐ Affirmative defenses (if any):
    • ☐ Lack of jurisdiction
    • ☐ Improper venue
    • ☐ Litis pendentia
    • ☐ Res judicata
    • ☐ Prescription
    • ☐ Failure to state cause of action
    • ☐ No barangay conciliation
  • ☐ Compulsory counterclaim (if any - MUST include or waived)
  • ☐ Permissive counterclaim (if any - with filing fee)
  • ☐ Cross-claim against co-defendants (if any)

Annexes:

  • ☐ Verification
  • ☐ Certification Against Forum Shopping
  • ☐ Supporting documents for defenses

C. Pre-Trial Checklist

Pre-Trial Brief (due 3 days before):

  • ☐ Statement of willingness to settle / submit to ADR
  • ☐ Summary of admitted facts
  • ☐ Proposed stipulations of facts
  • ☐ Issues to be tried
  • ☐ List of documents/exhibits (with copies attached)
  • ☐ List of witnesses with summary of expected testimony
  • ☐ Number of hours needed for presentation

Judicial Affidavits (due 5 days before):

  • ☐ All witness judicial affidavits prepared
  • ☐ Q&A format followed
  • ☐ Properly notarized
  • ☐ Lawyer attestation included
  • ☐ Documentary exhibits attached

At Pre-Trial:

  • ☐ Counsel with SPA to enter into compromise
  • ☐ Principal party present (or validly excused)
  • ☐ Bring all original documents
  • ☐ Prepare marking of exhibits
  • ☐ List proposed stipulations

D. Appeal Checklist

CRITICAL: Meet the Deadline!

  • ☐ Note date of receipt of judgment
  • ☐ If filing MR: 15 days from notice
  • ☐ If MR denied: 15 days from denial (fresh period rule)
  • ☐ No MR: 15 days from judgment

Notice of Appeal (Rules 40, 41):

  • ☐ File with court a quo (trial court)
  • ☐ Pay appellate docket fees
  • ☐ State parties appealing
  • ☐ State judgment/order appealed from
  • ☐ State court to which appeal is taken

Petition for Review (Rules 42, 43, 45):

  • ☐ File with appellate court directly
  • ☐ Pay docket and other fees
  • ☐ Include material dates
  • ☐ Statement of matters involved
  • ☐ Questions raised
  • ☐ Verification
  • ☐ Certification against forum shopping
  • ☐ Certified true copy of judgment
  • ☐ Copies of relevant pleadings and documents

Rule 65 Certiorari:

  • ☐ STRICT 60-day period (non-extendible)
  • ☐ No appeal or adequate remedy available
  • ☐ Show grave abuse of discretion
  • ☐ Certified true copies of judgment/order
  • ☐ All relevant pleadings and documents

E. Execution Checklist

Pre-Execution:

  • ☐ Confirm judgment is final and executory
  • ☐ Entry of judgment made
  • ☐ No pending appeal/MR
  • ☐ Within 5 years from entry (for motion)

Motion for Execution:

  • ☐ File motion with court that rendered judgment
  • ☐ Attach certified copy of judgment
  • ☐ Attach entry of judgment
  • ☐ Identify properties for levy (if known)

Post-Writ:

  • ☐ Sheriff demands immediate payment
  • ☐ If unpaid: levy on personal property first
  • ☐ If insufficient: levy on real property
  • ☐ Publication requirements met (real property)
  • ☐ Public auction properly noticed
  • ☐ Sheriff's return filed

Watch for:

  • ☐ Third-party claims (terceria)
  • ☐ Exempt properties
  • ☐ Redemption rights (real property - 1 year)
