PART I: SMALL CLAIMS CASES

Legal Basis: A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC (2008), as amended by A.M. No. 21-04-17-SC (2021)

1. Overview and Purpose

WHAT IS SMALL CLAIMS?

A special civil action designed to provide an inexpensive and expeditious means to settle disputes involving small sums of money without the need for formal rules of procedure and legal representation.

KEY FEATURES:
  • NO LAWYERS allowed during hearing
  • NO formal pleadings - prescribed forms only
  • SAME DAY decision or within 5 days
  • FINAL and UNAPPEALABLE decision
  • NO barangay conciliation required
  • Reduced filing fees

3. Venue Rules

Type of Defendant Where to File
Individual Defendant At the option of plaintiff:
  • Where plaintiff resides; OR
  • Where defendant resides
Two or More Defendants with Different Residences At the option of plaintiff:
  • Where plaintiff resides; OR
  • Where ANY of the defendants resides
Juridical Entity (Corporation, Partnership) At the option of plaintiff:
  • Where plaintiff resides; OR
  • Where defendant's principal office is located; OR
  • Where defendant's branch office is located (if transaction occurred there)
Non-Resident Defendant Where plaintiff resides

  • Venue cannot be waived - court may dismiss motu proprio if improperly filed
  • Barangay conciliation is NOT required before filing

4. Commencement of Action

A. How to File

1PREPARE THE STATEMENT OF CLAIM

  • Use the prescribed Form 1-SCC (Statement of Claim)
  • Fill out completely and legibly
  • State the cause of action clearly
  • Indicate the amount of claim
  • Attach supporting documents

2PREPARE SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS

Attach certified photocopies of:

  • Contract, promissory note, or written agreement
  • Receipts, vouchers, or proof of payment
  • Demand letter (if any)
  • Barangay certification (if applicable)
  • Any other relevant document

3FILE WITH THE COURT

  • File with the proper MTC/MCTC/MeTC/MTCC
  • Pay the required filing fees (reduced rates for small claims)
  • Submit sufficient copies for all parties

4COURT ACTION (within 24 hours)

  • If sufficient in form and substance ‚Üí Court issues Summons
  • If insufficient ‚Üí Court dismisses outright OR orders amendment within non-extendible period
  • Court sets hearing date (not earlier than 15 days but not later than 30 days from filing)

C. Effect of Non-Appearance

Who is Absent Effect
PLAINTIFF absent
(without justifiable cause)
  • Claim is DISMISSED without prejudice
  • May refile the claim
  • Counterclaim (if any) may proceed
DEFENDANT absent
(without justifiable cause)
  • Effect of submission to judgment
  • Court decides based on evidence of plaintiff
  • Defendant waives right to present evidence
BOTH absent Case DISMISSED without prejudice

D. Hearing Procedure (Step-by-Step)

1PRELIMINARY CONFERENCE

Court shall:

  • Clarify issues
  • Determine facts admitted
  • Explore possibility of amicable settlement

2MEDIATION ATTEMPT

  • Court actively encourages settlement
  • If settled ‚Üí Judgment based on Compromise
  • Settlement is immediately final and executory

3PRESENTATION OF EVIDENCE (If no settlement)

  • Plaintiff presents claim and evidence
  • Defendant presents defense and evidence
  • Court may ask clarificatory questions
  • Rules on evidence applied in suppletory character
  • No formal offer of evidence required

4DECISION

  • Rendered on SAME DAY of hearing; OR
  • Within 5 DAYS from hearing if court needs to evaluate
  • Decision is FINAL and IMMEDIATELY EXECUTORY

Aspect Rule

Appellate Court Regional Trial Court
Appeal Bond Not required (unless execution pending appeal)

Special Rule for Ejectment Cases

SUPERSEDEAS BOND (To Stay Execution)

In ejectment cases, to STAY EXECUTION pending appeal, defendant must:

  1. Perfect the appeal
  2. File a SUPERSEDEAS BOND to answer for:
    • Rents
    • Damages
    • Costs accruing down to the time of judgment
  3. Deposit MONTHLY RENTALS as they fall due during pendency of appeal

Failure to comply = Immediate execution

PART V: CHECKLISTS

A. Checklist: Filing Small Claims Case

BEFORE FILING:
  • Claim is ‚â§ ‚Ç±1,000,000
  • Claim is for money only (not real property, not ejectment)
  • Claim is covered (loan, lease, services, sale of personal property, etc.)
  • Determined proper venue (residence of plaintiff or defendant)
  • NOT against the government
  • NO provisional remedy needed
DOCUMENTS TO PREPARE:
  • Statement of Claim (Form 1-SCC) - completely filled out
  • Contract/Agreement/Promissory Note (certified copy)
  • Receipts or proof of payment/non-payment
  • Demand letter and proof of service (if any)
  • Computation of claim
  • Valid ID of plaintiff
  • Authority document (if corporation/through representative)
  • Filing fee
AT THE HEARING:
  • Appear PERSONALLY (no lawyer)
  • Bring original documents
  • Bring witnesses (if needed)
  • Be prepared to settle
  • Be ready to present your case simply and clearly

B. Checklist: Defending Small Claims Case

UPON RECEIVING SUMMONS:
  • Note the hearing date
  • Prepare Response (Form 2-SCC) within 10 days
  • Gather evidence to support defense
  • Prepare counterclaim if applicable (compulsory only)
DOCUMENTS FOR DEFENSE:
  • Response (Form 2-SCC) - completely filled out
  • Proof of payment (if claiming payment made)
  • Contract showing different terms (if disputing amount)
  • Documents showing defects/non-performance (if applicable)
  • Computation of counterclaim (if any)
  • Valid ID
AT THE HEARING:
  • Appear PERSONALLY (no lawyer)
  • Bring original documents
  • Be prepared to settle
  • Present defense clearly

C. Checklist: Filing Ejectment Case (Summary Procedure)

BEFORE FILING:
  • Determined type: Forcible Entry OR Unlawful Detainer
  • Within 1-year period from dispossession/last demand
  • Barangay conciliation completed (if required)
  • Demand to vacate served (for unlawful detainer)
DOCUMENTS TO PREPARE:
  • Verified Complaint
  • Contract of Lease (if unlawful detainer based on lease)
  • Proof of ownership/right to possess
  • Demand letter to vacate with proof of service
  • Barangay Certificate to File Action
  • Tax Declaration (if claiming ownership)
  • Affidavits of witnesses
  • Filing fee
PERIODS TO REMEMBER:
  • Answer: 10 days from summons
  • Preliminary Conference: Within 30 days from last pleading
  • Position Paper: 10 days from conference
  • Decision: 30 days from submission
  • Appeal: 15 days from judgment
