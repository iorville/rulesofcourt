PHASE 0: PRE-LITIGATION REQUIREMENTS

Katarungang Pambarangay (Barangay Conciliation)

Legal Basis: Local Government Code, Sections 399-422

MANDATORY WHEN:
  • Parties actually reside in the same city/municipality
  • Dispute involves real property located in the same city/municipality

Exceptions - When Barangay Conciliation is NOT Required

Category Exceptions (May file DIRECTLY in court)
A. Parties-Based
  • One party is the government or any subdivision/instrumentality
  • One party is a public officer or employee (in official capacity)
  • One party is a juridical entity (corporation, partnership, etc.)
  • Parties reside in different cities/municipalities
  • Party is a non-resident of the city/municipality
B. Nature of Action
  • Actions coupled with PROVISIONAL REMEDIES (preliminary injunction, attachment, replevin, support pendente lite, receivership)
  • Actions which may be barred by statute of limitations
  • Labor disputes
  • Where URGENT LEGAL ACTION is needed to prevent injustice, fraud, or preserve evidence
C. Criminal Cases
  • Offense punishable by imprisonment exceeding 1 year
  • Offense punishable by fine exceeding ₱5,000
  • Offenses where there is no private offended party
  • Offense committed by public officer in relation to office
D. Special Cases
  • Cases not conciliated within time limit (automatic certification)
  • Repudiation of settlement (within 10 days)
  • Where venue is expressly fixed by law (e.g., ejectment)

Barangay Conciliation Process Flow

1. Filing with Punong Barangay → 2. Mediation (15 days) → 3. Pangkat Conciliation (15 days) → 4. Settlement / Certification / Arbitration

TOTAL MAXIMUM PERIOD: 60 days from filing (may be extended by agreement)

Effects of Non-Compliance

Situation Effect
Case filed without Certification to File Action May be DISMISSED (without prejudice); must be raised by defendant; waived if not raised in Answer
Amicable Settlement reached Has force of final judgment; enforceable by execution within 6 months by Lupon; after 6 months → file action in court
Repudiation of Settlement Must be done within 10 DAYS; grounds: vitiated consent (fraud, violence, intimidation)

SPECIAL COURTS

Sandiganbayan (Anti-Graft Court)

Legal Basis: RA 8249, as amended by RA 10660

Aspect Details
Jurisdiction Over
  • RA 3019 (Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act)
  • RA 1379 (Forfeiture of Unlawfully Acquired Property)
  • Bribery, Indirect Bribery, Corruption of Public Officials (RPC)
  • Sequestration cases (ill-gotten wealth)
Officials Covered
(Salary Grade 27+)
  • Executive branch officials (regional directors and above)
  • Members of Congress, Judiciary, Constitutional Commissions
  • Ambassadors, public ministers, consuls
  • Military: Colonels/Naval Captains and above
  • PNP: Chief Superintendent and above
  • Provincial governors, vice governors, board members
  • City mayors/council members (highly urbanized/independent cities)
  • GOCC/GFI presidents, directors, trustees, managers
Exclusions
  • Public officers with salary grade BELOW 27 → RTC
  • Private individuals acting alone
Appeal Direct to Supreme Court (Rule 45) - Questions of law only

Family Courts

Legal Basis: RA 8369 (Family Courts Act of 1997)

Case Type Covered Cases
Criminal Cases
  • Cases where accused is below 18 at time of commission
  • RA 7610 (Child Abuse Law) violations
  • RA 9262 (Anti-VAWC Law) violations
  • RA 9208 (Anti-Trafficking) involving children
Civil Cases
  • Guardianship, custody of children
  • Adoption
  • Annulment of marriage / Declaration of nullity
  • Legal separation
  • Support and acknowledgment
  • Property relations of spouses
  • Parental authority, legitimacy, filiation
Petitions
  • Habeas Corpus involving custody of minors
  • Protection Orders (TPO, PPO) under RA 9262
  • Declaration of child as abandoned/dependent/neglected
  • Suspension/termination of parental authority
Special Rules
  • Mediation MANDATORY before trial (except VAWC)
  • Social worker assessment required
  • Child-friendly procedures
  • Closed-door hearings for minors
Note: Where no Family Court exists, designated RTC branch handles these cases.

Court of Tax Appeals (CTA)

Legal Basis: RA 1125, as amended by RA 9282 and RA 9503

CTA Division CTA En Banc
Original Jurisdiction:
  • CIR decisions on disputed assessments, refunds
  • CIR inaction on disputed assessment/refund claim
  • COC decisions on customs duties, seizure
  • Secretary of Finance decisions on customs
  • RTC decisions on local tax cases
  • CBAA decisions on real property tax
  • Tax collection cases (principal > ₱1,000,000)
  • Criminal violations of NIRC and CMTA
 Appellate Jurisdiction:
  • Appeals from CTA Division decisions
  • Period: 15 days from notice
Appeal Periods to CTA:
  • From CIR/COC decision: 30 DAYS from receipt
  • From CIR inaction: Before lapse of 180 days OR within 30 days from lapse
  • From RTC decision: 15 DAYS from notice
Appeals from CTA en banc: Direct to Supreme Court (Rule 45)

Shari'a Courts

Legal Basis: PD 1083 (Code of Muslim Personal Laws)

Shari'a Circuit Courts (First Level) Shari'a District Courts (Second Level)
  • Offenses under Code of Muslim Personal Laws
  • Civil actions between Muslims relating to:
    • Marriage, divorce, betrothal
    • Customary dower (mahr)
    • Property disposition upon divorce
    • Maintenance and support
  • Amount: ≤ ₱200,000
  • Same matters as Circuit Court
  • Amount: > ₱200,000
  • Custody, guardianship, paternity, filiation
  • Petitions for declaration of absence
  • Petitions for hajr (guardianship of insane)
  • Appeals from Shari'a Circuit Courts
Important Limitations:
  • BOTH parties must be Muslims
  • If one party is non-Muslim → Regular courts have jurisdiction
  • Marriage must have been contracted under Muslim law
Appeals: Shari'a District Courts → Directly to Supreme Court

PHASE 2: PLEADINGS STAGE

The Complaint (Rule 6)

Component Requirements
Caption Full names of parties, designation (Plaintiff/Defendant), docket number
Body Allegations constituting cause of action, ultimate facts, specific relief sought
Verification Affiant read and understood contents; allegations true based on personal knowledge or authentic records
Certification Against Forum Shopping Executed by PARTY (not counsel); states no other pending action; undertakes to inform court of similar actions
Attachments Actionable documents, written instruments, Secretary's Certificate (corporations), SPA (if representative), Certificate to File Action (if barangay required)
Effects of Non-Compliance:
  • No Verification: Pleading treated as unsigned; may be stricken; court may allow amendment
  • No Certification: Cause for DISMISSAL
  • False Certification: DISMISSAL with prejudice + contempt
  • Signed by Counsel Only: Generally NOT curable (dismissal)

Summons (Rule 14)

Mode of Service When Used
1. Personal Service ALWAYS preferred; handed to defendant in person
2. Substituted Service If personal service impossible within reasonable time; at residence (suitable age/discretion) or office (competent person)
3. Publication Defendant unknown/cannot be found; non-resident; actions in rem/quasi in rem; once weekly for 3 consecutive weeks
4. Extraterritorial Non-resident defendant; action affects personal status or property in Philippines
5. Electronic Via email/electronic means; requires court authorization (2019 Amendment)
When Summons NOT Required:
  • Voluntary Appearance: Filing any pleading (except MTD for lack of jurisdiction over person)
  • Actions in Rem: Service by publication sufficient
  • Estoppel: Defendant participated actively in trial
  • Already in Court: Counterclaim/Cross-claim (no summons needed)

Defendant's Response

Document Period
Answer (RTC/MTC) 30 CALENDAR DAYS from summons
Answer to Amended Complaint 15 calendar days from service
Answer to Counterclaim 15 calendar days from service
Answer to Cross-claim 15 calendar days from service
Answer to Third-Party Complaint 30 calendar days from summons
Reply (if any) 15 calendar days from answer

Motion to Dismiss - LIMITED GROUNDS (2019 Rules)

Only these grounds may be raised in a Motion to Dismiss:
  1. Lack of jurisdiction over SUBJECT MATTER (cannot be waived)
  2. Pending action between same parties (LITIS PENDENTIA)
  3. RES JUDICATA
  4. PRESCRIPTION
  5. Non-compliance with BARANGAY CONCILIATION

ALL OTHER GROUNDS → Must be raised as AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES in Answer

Default (Rule 9, Sec. 3)

Timing Remedy
Before Judgment Motion to Set Aside Order of Default (under oath, show FAME, meritorious defense)
After Judgment, Before Finality Motion for New Trial (FAME or newly discovered evidence) - 15 days from notice; OR Appeal (limited to lack of jurisdiction or excessive judgment)
After Finality Petition for Relief (within 60 days from knowledge, not more than 6 months from entry); OR Annulment of Judgment (extrinsic fraud or lack of jurisdiction)

PHASE 3: PRE-TRIAL STAGE

Court-Annexed Mediation (CAM) & Judicial Dispute Resolution (JDR)

Stage Duration Conducted By Outcome
CAM
(A.M. No. 01-10-5-SC-PHILJA)		 30 days (extendible 30 days) PMC Mediator Settlement → Judgment on Compromise
No Settlement → Proceed to JDR
JDR
(A.M. No. 11-1-6-SC-PHILJA)		 30 days JDR Judge (different from trial judge) Settlement → Judgment on Compromise
No Settlement → Pre-Trial proper
Cases NOT Referred to Mediation:
  • Civil cases under Summary Procedure (except damages)
  • Cases where party is infant/incompetent or detained
  • Habeas corpus
  • Cases involving government as party
  • Cases where restraining order/injunction is sought
  • Cases with allegations of violence under RA 9262
  • Cases already mediated but failed
  • Cases where issue is purely legal

Pre-Trial Conference (Rule 18)

Requirement Details
Pre-Trial Brief Filed at least 3 DAYS before pre-trial
Contents
  • Statement of willingness to settle/ADR
  • Summary of admitted facts and proposed stipulations
  • Issues to be tried
  • Documents/exhibits (with copies)
  • List of witnesses with summary of testimony
  • Number of trial dates needed
Attendance Parties must personally appear OR authorized representative with SPA (specific, with authority to compromise)
Effects of Non-Appearance:
  • Plaintiff absent: Case DISMISSED (with prejudice)
  • Defendant absent: Plaintiff presents evidence EX PARTE
  • No Pre-Trial Brief: Same effect as non-appearance

PHASE 4: TRIAL STAGE

Judicial Affidavit Rule (A.M. No. 12-8-8-SC)

MANDATORY in all courts EXCEPT Supreme Court and Court of Appeals
Aspect Requirement
What It Is Direct testimony in Question and Answer form
Contents
  • Name, age, residence, occupation of witness
  • Name and address of lawyer
  • Statement that affiant is answering under oath
  • Q&A format testimony
  • Signature of witness, Jurat
  • Lawyer's attestation
Filing Period 5 DAYS before pre-trial (with exhibits)
Effect at Trial Judicial affidavit = DIRECT EXAMINATION (no oral direct); witness confirms and is cross-examined
Non-Compliance Witness shall NOT be allowed to testify
Exceptions to Judicial Affidavit Requirement:
  • Small Claims cases
  • Criminal cases under Summary Procedure
  • Child witnesses (may testify in open court with safeguards)
  • Hostile/adverse witnesses
  • Witnesses who cannot read/write
  • Rebuttal/Sur-rebuttal witnesses (if matter not anticipated)

Order of Trial (Rule 30)

# Stage Notes
1 Plaintiff's Case-in-Chief Present witnesses (cross-exam by defendant); mark and identify evidence
2 Defendant's Case-in-Chief Present witnesses (cross-exam by plaintiff); mark and identify evidence
3 Plaintiff's Rebuttal Evidence to refute defendant's evidence
4 Defendant's Sur-Rebuttal Evidence to refute plaintiff's rebuttal
5 Formal Offer of Evidence IN WRITING within 5 days after testimony; Opposition: 5 days; Ruling: 5 days
6 Memoranda If required by court; 30 days from submission
7 Case Submitted for Decision -

Demurrer to Evidence (Rule 33)

Filed If Granted If Denied
WITHOUT Leave of Court Case dismissed Defendant WAIVES right to present evidence
WITH Leave of Court Case dismissed Defendant may still present evidence

PHASE 5: JUDGMENT

Period to Decide (Constitutional Mandate)

Court Period from Submission
First Level Courts (MTC) 90 DAYS
Regional Trial Courts 90 DAYS
Court of Appeals 12 MONTHS
Supreme Court 24 MONTHS

Types of Judgment

Type When Applicable
Judgment on the Pleadings (Rule 34) Answer fails to tender an issue; admits material allegations
Summary Judgment (Rule 35) No genuine issue of material fact; motion filed at least 60 days before trial
Default Judgment Defendant failed to answer and declared in default
Judgment on Compromise Based on settlement agreement; has effect of res judicata
Judgment after Trial Full adjudication on merits

Finality of Judgment

Judgment becomes FINAL and EXECUTORY:
  • 15 days from RECEIPT of judgment (if no MR or appeal)
  • 15 days from RECEIPT of denial of MR/Motion for New Trial
  • After appeal is exhausted and affirmed

PHASE 7: EXECUTION OF JUDGMENT

Types of Execution

Type When Period
Execution as a Matter of Right Judgment is FINAL and EXECUTORY Upon motion
Execution by Motion After finality Within 5 YEARS from entry of judgment
Execution by Action After 5 years Within 10 YEARS from finality
Execution Pending Appeal Before finality; requires "good reasons" Upon motion with bond

Enforcement Process (Money Judgments)

Step Action
1 Demand - Sheriff demands immediate payment
2 Levy on Personal Property - If no payment
3 Levy on Real Property - If personal property insufficient
4 Execution Sale - Notice 20 days (personal) / 20 days + published 3 weeks (real); public auction
5 Redemption Period - Real property: 1 YEAR from registration of sale
6 Consolidation - If no redemption, buyer gets final deed

Properties Exempt from Execution

  • Family home (except debts for construction/purchase)
  • Wearing apparel and necessaries; ordinary household furniture
  • Tools and implements for trade (up to ₱300,000)
  • Professional libraries and equipment (up to ₱300,000)
  • One horse, cow, carabao or beast of burden
  • Provisions for support (3 months)
  • Salary/wages for personal services within 4 months preceding levy
  • Letters and personal writings
  • Burial lots (not for profit)
  • Properties in custodia legis
  • Benefits under SSS, GSIS, PAG-IBIG

Note: Exemptions must be CLAIMED - not automatic

SPECIAL PROCEEDINGS (Rules 72-109)

Proceeding Key Points
Settlement of Estate
(Rules 73-91)
  • Testate (with will) / Intestate (no will)
  • Venue: Province where decedent resided
  • Must file within 30 days (if executor)
  • Publication: Once weekly for 3 weeks
Guardianship
(Rules 92-97)
  • Of minors or incompetents
  • Venue: Residence of minor/incompetent
  • Bond required; annual accounting
Habeas Corpus
(Rule 102)
  • Remedy for illegal detention
  • Return: Within 3 days (immediately if urgent)
  • Hearing: Within 2 days from return
  • SC/CA/RTC concurrent jurisdiction
Change of Name
(Rule 103)
  • Publication: Once weekly for 3 weeks
  • Venue: RTC where petitioner resides
  • OSG must be notified
Correction of Entries
(Rule 108)
  • Venue: RTC where civil registry is located
  • Publication required; Civil Registrar as party
  • Administrative Remedy (RA 9048/10172):
    • Clerical errors, first name → Local Civil Registrar
    • Sex, birth date → Civil Registrar General
